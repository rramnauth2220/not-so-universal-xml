Harrington<br/>Vice President, Regulatory Affairs</p><p>Ladies and Gentlemen:</p><p>1. On March 25, 2014, Tallgrass Interstate Gas Transmission, LLC (Tallgrass) filed a Petition for a Limited Waiver of Tariff  Provisions (Petition). Tallgrass seeks a limited and short-term  waiver of its FERC Gas Tariff,  Fifth Revised Volume  No. 1 (Tariff)  to allow it to resolve a gas imbalance  with Laclede Gas Company, Missouri Gas Energy Division (Laclede). Tallgrass states that the imbalance  was caused by a meter  error and that it proposes to resolve the situation by means of a volumetric  payback  rather than through the cash-out  provisions in accordance with section 5.2 of its Firm Transportation Rate Schedule (FTRS) Tariff  provisions.</p><p>2. Tallgrass states that section 5.2 of its FTRS Tariff  governs the monthly  balancing provisions, including cash-out  provisions related to its shipper  month end imbalances.  Tallgrass states that section 5.2d(6) of the FTRS provides that<page number="2" pageScheme="2014 FERC LEXIS 596"/> "Transporter will charge for or credit for any shipper  imbalances  remaining after the imbalance  netting and trading  procedures set out in subsections (1) through (5) above..." Tallgrass explains that this section also provides for the determination of specific gas prices based on a credit or charge imbalance  position to be applied to the net imbalance  volumes  after trading  and is applied to the shipper's  monthly  invoice amount due.</p><p>3. Tallgrass explains that due to the meter  error at the delivery  meter  between its system and that of Laclede, gas flowed  in excess of Laclede's nominations  for approximately 21 hours between February 3 and February 4, 2014. <footnote><anchor id="fnote1_13_192_116"/><label>1</label><bodyText><p> According to Tallgrass, low pressure on its system caused the meter  to "over-range", thereby flowing 17,670 Dth of excess gas above the nominated   volume  while concurrently under-measuring the actual quantity that flowed. </p></bodyText></footnote> As a result, continues Tallgrass, Laclede's nominations  were less than the actual gas flow at the meter,  causing a short imbalance  position of 21,692 Dth on Laclede's transportation contract. <footnote><anchor id="fnote2_13_201_122"/><label>2</label><bodyText><p> Tallgrass explains that a short imbalance  position on its system is created by a shipper  nominating  for less than the actual gas that flowed  through the delivery  meter,  thereby creating a situation where the shipper  owes Tallgrass for gas flowed  but not nominated.  Tallgrass adds that Laclede's month-end short imbalance  position is rectified through Tallgrass' "Credit" cash-out  provision using the 0-5 percent imbalance  position and 100 percent spot  gas pricing pursuant to section 5.2d(6) of the FTRS.</p></bodyText></footnote> Tallgrass also states that Laclede was not aware of this imbalance  position until after February's production month and therefore was unable to rectify  the imbalance  through nominations  adjustments in February. According to Tallgrass, absent the meter  error, Laclede's actual short imbalance  position would have been approximately 4,020<page number="3" pageScheme="2014 FERC LEXIS 596"/> Dth.</p><p>4. Tallgrass points out that, in accordance with section 5.2d(6) of the FTRS, the cash-out  price for the February 2014 imbalance  short position is $ 13.76 per Dth. <footnote><anchor id="fnote3_16_287_155"/><label>3</label><bodyText><p> Tallgrass explains that the $ 13.76 price is the applicable cash out price per tariff  and equal to the Panhandle Eastern (PEPL) highest weekly spot  gas price delivered as published by <emphasis typeStyle="it">Natural Gas Intelligence</emphasis> for February 2014.</p></bodyText></footnote> Tallgrass adds that the gas prices in February were atypically high due to severe cold weather conditions throughout<page number="4" pageScheme="2014 FERC LEXIS 596"/> the Midwest.</p><p>5. Tallgrass asserts that the impact of the meter  error and February gas prices has created a situation that would cause Laclede to face a considerable imbalance  payback  dollar amount, even though it did not have the information required to manage and minimize its imbalance.  Tallgrass emphasizes that the meter  error impacted  only Laclede and no other shippers.  Further, continues Tallgrass, because the meter  error was caused by operating conditions outside of Laclede's control and did not afford Laclede time to remedy the imbalance  by volumetric  payback  in February, Tallgrass proposes to remedy the imbalance  through a volumetric  payback  in April 2014 in lieu of the cash-out  provisions of its Tariff  that otherwise would apply to this February imbalance. </p><p>6. Accordingly, <page number="5" pageScheme="2014 FERC LEXIS 596"/> Tallgrass requests a limited and short-term  waiver of its Tariff  as necessary to allow it to rectify  Laclede's February gas imbalance  of 17,670 Dth caused by the meter  error through a volumetric  payback  in lieu of the cash-out  provisions of its FTRS. Tallgrass adds that the volumetric  payback  would be handled through Laclede nominating  excess volumes  at its delivery  point on the Tallgrass system during the month of April 2014. Tallgrass maintains that granting the waiver will allow it and Laclede to address an extraordinary situation and rectify  it in an equitable fashion. Tallgrass adds that it does not believe that any other shippers  will be adversely impacted  if the Commission grants the requested waiver.</p><p>7. Public notice of the Tallgrass filing was issued March 26, 2014. Interventions and protests  were due as provided in <citation type="regulation" id="I5C0NM302N1R530070000400"><content>section 154.210</content></citation> of the Commission's regulations (<citation type="regulation" id="I5C0NM302N1R530080000400"><content><span normalizedCite="18 C.F.R. § 154.210">18 C.F.R. § 154.210 (2013)<locator><locatorKey><keyName name="citeReference"/><keyValue value="#14829#123#000018#        154.210#"/></locatorKey></locator></span></content></citation>). Pursuant to Rule 214 (<citation type="regulation" id="I5C0NM302N1R5300B0000400"><content><span normalizedCite="18 C.F.R. § 385.214">18 C.F.R. § 385.214 (2013)<locator><locatorKey><keyName name="citeReference"/><keyValue value="#14829#123#000018#        385.214#"/></locatorKey></locator></span></content></citation>), all timely filed motions to intervene  and any unopposed motions to intervene  out-of-time filed before the issuance date of this order are granted. Granting late intervention at this stage of the proceeding will not disrupt this<page number="6" pageScheme="2014 FERC LEXIS 596"/> proceeding or place additional burdens on existing parties. No protest  or adverse comments were filed.</p><p>8. For good cause shown, the Commission grants the requested waiver of the applicable provisions of section 5.2 of the Tallgrass FTRS Tariff.  Tallgrass is authorized to resolve the meter-error portion of Laclede's February 2014 gas imbalance  by means of a volumetric  payback  in lieu of the cash-out  provisions of the FTRS.</p><p>By On December 12, 2013, and December 20, 2013, and supplemented on January 14, 2014, Idaho Power Company, licensee  for the Bliss Hydropower Project, FERC No. 1975, <footnote><anchor id="fnote1_7_73_59"/><label>1</label><bodyText><p><emphasis typeStyle="it">See</emphasis><citation type="agencyDecision" id="I5C0NM3B28T4CT0020000400"><content>Order Issuing New License, <span normalizedCite="108 F.E.R.C. P 61128">108 FERC P 61,128<locator><locatorKey><keyName name="citeReference"/><keyValue value="#18#3#000108#061128#"/></locatorKey></locator></span></content></citation> (issued August 4, 2004).</p></bodyText></footnote> filed revised  Exhibit G drawings  pursuant of ordering  paragraph (B) of an Order Modifying and Approving Land Management Plan Updates (Order). <footnote><anchor id="fnote2_7_82_205"/><label>2</label><bodyText><p><citation type="agencyDecision" id="I5C0NM3B28T4CT0060000400"><content><span normalizedCite="143 F.E.R.C. P 62069">143 FERC P 62,069<locator><locatorKey><keyName name="citeReference"/><keyValue value="#18#3#000143#062069#"/></locatorKey></locator></span></content></citation> (issued April 29, 2013).</p></bodyText></footnote> The project is located on the Snake River in Gooding County, Twin Falls, and Elmore Counties, Idaho. The project occupies  federal lands managed by the Bureau of Land Management (BLM).</p><p><emphasis typeStyle="un"><emphasis typeStyle="bf">Background</emphasis></emphasis></p><p>2. Ordering  paragraph (B) of the Order, in part, requires the licensee,  within 90 days of the date of the Order, to file for Commission approval revised  Exhibit G drawings  incorporating the access road  to the Bliss power plant  from the end of the paved  country road at<page number="2" pageScheme="2014 FERC LEXIS 597"/> the top  of the canyon  into the project boundary. The drawings  must comply with <citation type="regulation" id="I5C0NG6P25CK000040000400"><content>sections 4.39</content></citation> and <citation type="regulation" id="I5C0NG8H0K1MWM0010000400"><content>4.41</content></citation> of the Commission's regulations.  The due date to file the revised  drawings  was extended to January 29, 2014. <footnote><anchor id="fnote3_11_225_37"/><label>3</label><bodyText><p><emphasis typeStyle="it">See</emphasis> Unpublished Order Granting Extensions of Time (issued July 1, 2013).</p></bodyText></footnote></p><p><emphasis typeStyle="un"><emphasis typeStyle="bf">Discussion</emphasis></emphasis></p><p>3. Our review of the revised  Exhibit G drawings  filed on December 12 and 20, 2013, and supplemented on January 14, 2014, found that the drawings  incorporate the access road  to the Bliss power plant  from the end of the paved  county road at the top  of the canyon  into the project boundary. We also found discrepancies in the amount of federal land  acreage  occupied by the transmission line  and non-transmission  line lands. The revised  Exhibit G drawings  identify 46.95 acres  of federal land  occupied by the transmission line,  and 114.7 acres  of federal land  for other than the use of transmission  lines. However, Article 201, as amended, identifies 104.08 acres  of federal land  for project transmission <page number="3" pageScheme="2014 FERC LEXIS 597"/> lines, <footnote><anchor id="fnote4_14_309_22"/><label>4</label><bodyText><p><emphasis typeStyle="it">See</emphasis> unpublished Errata Notice: Order Deleting Exhibit G Drawings  Issued November 23, 2005 (issued December 13, 2005).</p></bodyText></footnote> and 183.95 acres  of federal land  for other than the use of transmission  lines. <footnote><anchor id="fnote5_14_318_60"/><label>5</label><bodyText><p> Order Approving Revised  Exhibit G Drawings  and Revising Annual Charges. <citation type="agencyDecision" id="I5C0NM3B28T4CT00D0000400"><content><span normalizedCite="112 F.E.R.C. P 62198">112 FERC P 62,198<locator><locatorKey><keyName name="citeReference"/><keyValue value="#18#3#000112#062198#"/></locatorKey></locator></span></content></citation> (issued September 6, 2005).</p></bodyText></footnote> Therefore, on February 3, 2014, we requested the licensee  to explain the cause of discrepancy between the Exhibit A, Exhibit G, and the proposed revisions to the Exhibit G drawings.  In a March 12, 2014 filing, the licensee  states the revised  exhibits are based on better mapping data. The drawings  submitted conform to the Commission's rules and regulations  and are approved by this order, as shown in ordering  paragraph (A). In ordering  paragraph (B), we are requiring the licensee  to file the approved exhibit drawings  in aperture  card  and electronic  file formats. </p><p>4. Our review of the revised  Exhibit G drawings  and acceptance of the revised  acreage  of federal<page number="4" pageScheme="2014 FERC LEXIS 597"/> lands within the Bliss Project Boundary requires us to revise  the annual charges under Article 201 of the license. The licensee  agrees with this assessment. Ordering  paragraph (C) of this order revises  Article 201 (B) to reflect the correct acreage  of federal land  within the project boundary located within the transmission line  corridor to 46.95 acres.  This order also revises  Article 201(C) to reflect the correct acreage  of federal lands within the project boundary that occupy  non-transmission  line lands to 114.66 acres. </p><p>5. Based upon our review of the federal acreage  identified in the Exhibit G drawings,  we find the licensee's  Exhibit A must be revised  to reflect the corrected amount of federal lands within the project boundary. Ordering  paragraph (D) requires the licensee  to re-file a revised  Exhibit A to correctly reflect the amount of federal lands within the project boundary.</p><p>6. The Public Power Parties, in contrast, ask the Commission to affirm Bonneville's segmentation  policy and to deny the Powerex and Iberdrola protests) . The Public Power Parties first assert that Bonneville's segmentation  policy is beyond the scope of the Commission's review which is limited to the standards of section 7(a)(2) of the Northwest Power Act,  <footnote><anchor id="fnote8_22_337_194"/><label>8</label><bodyText><p><citation type="statute" id="I5C0NM4W2N1R6900C0000400"><content><span normalizedCite="16 U.S.C. § 839e (a)(2)">16 U.S.C. § 839e(a)(2) (2012)<locator><locatorKey><keyName name="citeReference"/><keyValue value="#1828#123#000016#       839e#"/></locatorKey></locator></span></content></citation>.</p></bodyText></footnote> and does not allow the Commission to consider the reasonableness of the design of Bonneville's rates. They add that, even if the Commission were able to review that policy, it appropriately allocates  costs and should be affirmed. <footnote><anchor id="fnote9_22_340_110"/><label>9</label><bodyText><p> Public Power Parties Comment at 11-14.</p></bodyText></footnote> The Western Agencies filed comments asking the Commission to deny Powerex's protest  against Bonneville's segmentation  policy because that policy equitably  allocates  costs. <footnote><anchor id="fnote10_22_352_11"/><label>10</label><bodyText><p> Western Agencies Comment at 11, 14.</p></bodyText></footnote></p><p>7. On November 14, 2013, Bonneville filed a reply  to the comments by the Joint IOUs, Western Agencies<page number="6" pageScheme="2014 FERC LEXIS 598"/> and Public Power Parties. Bonneville asserts that the Commission's jurisdiction to review its rates is limited to the standards set forth in the Northwest Power Act.  <footnote><anchor id="fnote11_26_391_136"/><label>11</label><bodyText><p> Bonneville Reply  at 3-5.</p></bodyText></footnote> Bonneville also asserts that it analyzed and considered all parties' arguments before coming to a final decision that its proposed segmentation,  including a 34.5 kV voltage  threshold for separating facilities and costs, equitably  allocates  costs. <footnote><anchor id="fnote12_26_403_11"/><label>12</label><bodyText><p><emphasis typeStyle="it">Id</emphasis>. at 5-7.</p></bodyText></footnote></p><p>8. On November 18, 2013, Powerex filed a Reply  to the Comments by Western Agencies and the Public Power Parties. Powerex asserts that the Commission's review includes Bonneville's segmentation  determination. <footnote><anchor id="fnote13_29_424_59"/><label>13</label><bodyText><p> Powerex Reply  at 3.</p></bodyText></footnote> Powerex also asserts that Bonneville's segmentation  explanations are insufficient to demonstrate that transmission  costs are equitably  allocated. <footnote><anchor id="fnote14_29_433_53"/><label>14</label><bodyText><p><emphasis typeStyle="it">Id</emphasis>. at 10-11.</p></bodyText></footnote> Finally, Powerex argues that uniform transmission  rates for the network do not alone satisfy the equitable allocation standard. <footnote><anchor id="fnote15_29_436_82"/><label>15</label><bodyText><p><emphasis typeStyle="it">Id</emphasis>. at 8-10.</p></bodyText></footnote></p><p><page number="7" pageScheme="2014 FERC LEXIS 598"/></p><p><emphasis typeStyle="bf">III. <emphasis typeStyle="un">Discussion</emphasis></emphasis></p><p><emphasis typeStyle="bf">A. <emphasis typeStyle="un">Standard of Review</emphasis></emphasis></p><p>9. Under the Northwest Power Act,  the Commission's review of Bonneville's regional power and transmission  rates is limited to determining whether Bonneville's proposed rates meet the three specific requirements of section 7(a)(2) of the Northwest Power Act:  <footnote><anchor id="fnote16_35_479_4"/><label>16</label><bodyText><p><citation type="statute" id="I5C0NM4W2N1R6900W0000400"><content><span normalizedCite="16 U.S.C. § 839e (a)(2)">16 U.S.C. § 839e(a)(2) (2012)<locator><locatorKey><keyName name="citeReference"/><keyValue value="#1828#123#000016#       839e#"/></locatorKey></locator></span></content></citation>. Bonneville also must comply with the financial, accounting, and ratemaking requirements in Department of Energy Order No. RA 6120.2.</p></bodyText></footnote><excerpt><bodyText><p>(A) they must be sufficient to assure repayment  of the federal investment in the Federal Columbia River Power System over a reasonable number of years after first meeting Bonneville's other costs;</p><p>(B) they must be based upon Bonneville's total system costs; and</p><p>(C) insofar as transmission  rates are concerned, they must equitably   allocate  the costs of the federal transmission  system between federal and non-federal power.</p></bodyText></excerpt></p><p><page number="8" pageScheme="2014 FERC LEXIS 598"/></p><p>10. Commission review of Bonneville's non-regional, non-firm  rates also is limited. Review is restricted to determining whether such rates meet the requirements of section 7(k) of the Northwest Power Act,  <footnote><anchor id="fnote17_40_536_132"/><label>17</label><bodyText><p><citation type="statute" id="I5C0NM4W2N1R6900Y0000400"><content><span normalizedCite="16 U.S.C. § 839e (k)">16 U.S.C. § 839e(k) (2012)<locator><locatorKey><keyName name="citeReference"/><keyValue value="#1828#123#000016#       839e#"/></locatorKey></locator></span></content></citation>.</p></bodyText></footnote> which requires that they comply with the Bonneville Project Act, the Flood Control Act of 1944, and the Federal Columbia River Transmission  System Act (Transmission  System Act). Taken together, those statutes require that Bonneville's non-regional, non-firm  rates:<excerpt><bodyText><p>(A) recover the cost of generation and transmission  of such electric energy, including the amortization of investments in the power projects within a reasonable period;</p><p>(B) encourage the most widespread use of Bonneville power; and</p><p>(C) provide the lowest possible rates to consumers consistent with sound business principles.</p></bodyText></excerpt></p><p>11. Unlike the Commission's statutory authority under the Federal Power Act, the Commission's authority under <citation type="statute" id="I5C0NM4W2N1R690100000400"><content>sections 7(a)</content></citation> and <citation type="statute" id="I5C0NM4W2N1R690110000400"><content>7(k)</content></citation> of<page number="9" pageScheme="2014 FERC LEXIS 598"/> the Northwest Power Act  does not include the power to modify the rates. The responsibility for developing rates in the first instance is vested with Bonneville's Administrator. The rates are then submitted to the Commission for approval or disapproval. In this regard, the Commission's role can be viewed as an appellate one: to affirm or remand the rates submitted to it for review. <footnote><anchor id="fnote18_45_619_365"/><label>18</label><bodyText><p><emphasis typeStyle="it">E.g., </emphasis><citation type="agencyDecision" id="I5C0NM4W2N1R690120000400"><content>United States Department of Energy - Bonneville Power Administration, <span normalizedCite="67 F.E.R.C. P 61351">67 FERC P 61,351<locator><locatorKey><keyName name="citeReference"/><keyValue value="#18#3#000067#061351#"/></locatorKey></locator></span>, at 62,216-17 (1994)</content></citation>; <emphasis typeStyle="it">see also, e.g., </emphasis><citation type="case" id="I5C0NM4W2N1R690180000400"><content>Aluminum Co. of America v. Bonneville Power Administration, <span normalizedCite="903 F.2d 585">903 F.2d 585<locator><locatorKey><keyName name="citeReference"/><keyValue value="#350#3#000903#000585#"/></locatorKey></locator></span>, <span normalizedCite="903 F.2d 585">592-93<locator><locatorKey><keyName name="citeReference"/><keyValue value="#350#3#000903#000585#"/></locatorKey></locator></span> (9th Cir. 1989)</content></citation>.</p></bodyText></footnote></p><p><emphasis typeStyle="bf">B. <emphasis typeStyle="un">Analysis</emphasis></emphasis></p><p>12. With the foregoing principles in mind, we will approve  on a final basis Bonneville's proposed rates. Based upon Bonneville's filings with the Commission, including the power repayment  studies, we find that the revenues expected to be collected  under the proposed rates will be sufficient to recover Bonneville's total system costs, including<page number="10" pageScheme="2014 FERC LEXIS 598"/> recovery of the remaining federal investment with interest, over the repayment  period.</p><p>13. While <citation type="statute" id="I5C0NM4W2N1R6901C0000400"><content>section 7(a)</content></citation> of the Northwest Power Act  requires only that the federal investment be repaid sometime within a reasonable number of years, traditionally we have considered the repayment  period as 50 years. In addition, we have required that some reasonable intermediate level of repayment  should exist to ensure that repayment  will occur by the end of the fiftieth year.</p><p>14. The traditional measure of the adequacy of Bonneville's revenues has been the power repayment  study. Bonneville's power repayment  studies indicates that the revenues expected to be collected  under the proposed rates will be sufficient to recover Bonneville's total system costs, including the recovery of the remaining federal investment, with interest, over the repayment  period.</p><p>15. In sum, our review of Bonneville's power repayment  studies indicates that its proposed rates are consistent with <citation type="statute" id="I5C0NM4W2N1R6901D0000400"><content>sections 7(a)(2)(A)</content></citation> and <citation type="statute" id="I5C0NM4W2N1R6901F0000400"><content>(B)</content></citation> of the Northwest Power Act. </p><p>16. With regard to Iberdrola's and Powerex's challenges to Bonneville's segmentation  analysis, requesting  that the Commission remand the rates, and arguing<page number="11" pageScheme="2014 FERC LEXIS 598"/> that Bonneville relied primarily on a segmentation methodology  originally agreed to in a non-precedential settlement  adopted in Bonneville's 1996 rate case,  we find their arguments are, in fact, challenges to the design of Bonneville's rates, and are therefore beyond the scope of the Commission's authority. <footnote><anchor id="fnote19_52_821_157"/><label>19</label><bodyText><p><emphasis typeStyle="it">E.g., </emphasis><citation type="agencyDecision" id="I5C0NM4W2N1R6901G0000400"><content>United States Department of Energy - Bonneville Power Administration, <span normalizedCite="95 F.E.R.C. P 61082">95 FERC P 61,082<locator><locatorKey><keyName name="citeReference"/><keyValue value="#18#3#000095#061082#"/></locatorKey></locator></span>, at 61,244-45 &amp; n.12 (2001)</content></citation>; <emphasis typeStyle="it">accord </emphasis><citation type="agencyDecision" id="I5C0NM4W2N1R6901N0000400"><content>United States Department of Energy - Bonneville Power Administration, <span normalizedCite="100 F.E.R.C. P 61102">100 FERC P 61,102<locator><locatorKey><keyName name="citeReference"/><keyValue value="#18#3#000100#061102#"/></locatorKey></locator></span>, at P 11 (2002)</content></citation> (Bonneville has "relatively unfettered discretion to design rates").</p></bodyText></footnote></p><p><emphasis typeStyle="un">The Commission orders</emphasis>:</p><p>Bonneville's proposed wholesale  power and transmission  rates are hereby confirmed and approved on a final basis for the period October 1, 2013, through September 30, 2015.</p><p>By the
