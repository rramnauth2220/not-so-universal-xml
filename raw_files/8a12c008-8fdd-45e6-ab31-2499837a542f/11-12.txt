P62,148"/></p><p>1. On September 6, 2013, the City of Seattle, Washington (licensee)  filed its Quality Assurance  Project Plan (QAPP) for its Temperature  Attainment  Plan (TAP) <footnote><anchor id="fnote1_8_114_86"/><label>1</label><bodyText><p><emphasis typeStyle="it">See</emphasis> Project Settlement Agreement Exhibit 9, for the licensee's   Temperature   Attainment  Plan developed during the relicensing  of the project and accepted into the licensing order via the Washington Department of Ecology's section 401 water quality   certification. </p></bodyText></footnote> with the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission (Commission) pursuant to license  Article 401 of the Order Issuing New License  <footnote><anchor id="fnote2_8_120_34"/><label>2</label><bodyText><p><emphasis typeStyle="it">See</emphasis><citation type="agencyDecision" id="I59XV8V92SF7MJ0070000400"><content><span normalizedCite="142 F.E.R.C. P 62231">142 FERC P 62,231<locator><locatorKey><keyName name="citeReference"/><keyValue value="#18#3#000142#062231#"/></locatorKey></locator></span></content></citation> (issued March 20, 2013).</p></bodyText></footnote> for the Boundary Project. The licensee's  Plan was also filed to comply with condition 3.5 of Washington Department of Ecology's Section 401 water quality  certification  (license  Appendix A) and Condition 3.8 of the U.S. Forest Services' section 4(e) conditions (license  Appendix B) that were made a part of the license  through ordering paragraphs (D) and (E), respectively. The project is located on the Pend Oreille River, about 10 miles north of the city of Metaline Falls and one mile south of the U.S.-Canada border.</p><p><page number="2" pageScheme="2013 FERC LEXIS 2031"/></p><p>LICENSE  REQUIREMENTS</p><p>2. License  Article 401 requires the licensee  to file various plans and reports for Commission approval. In part, Article 401 (b) requires the licensee  to file a Quality Assurance  Project Plan for its TAP. The purpose of the QAPP is for the licensee  to propose activities necessary to ensure the thoroughness and accuracy  of the temperature  data collected in its implementation of the TAP. The QAPP proposes monitoring  protocols,  quality assurance  procedures and quality control  specifications for temperature  monitoring. </p><p>BACKGROUND</p><p>3. During the relicensing  process, the licensee  developed a Temperature  Attainment  Plan in accordance with the requirements of the Washington Department of Ecology's (Ecology) 401 Water Quality  Certification  (WQC) which was, upon issuance  of the license,  accepted into the requirements of the license.  Article 401 (b) then required the licensee  to file a Quality Assurance  Project Plan for the Temperature  Attainment  Plan. The licensee's  current filing addressed in this<page number="3" pageScheme="2013 FERC LEXIS 2031"/> order is solely related to the Commission's approval of the QAPP, as the Temperature  Attainment  Plan has already been approved and accepted by the license  order, through acceptance of the WQC as a requirement of the license. </p><p><page number="64296" pageScheme="145 F.E.R.C. P62,148"/> LICENSEE'S  PLAN</p><p>4. The licensee's  QAPP describes its proposed approach for implementing the temperature  monitoring  portion of the TAP. The licensee  states that the temperature  objectives for the project are to implement measures to improve aquatic  habitat  as defined in the TAP and to measure any positive effects on water temperature  created by these improvements. The potential improvement of project water temperatures  is guided by temperature  attainment  goals defined in the WQC. <footnote><anchor id="fnote3_16_343_29"/><label>3</label><bodyText><p> In 2003, Ecology adopted designated  beneficial  uses in the mainstem Pend Oreille River from the River Mile 16 to River Mile 87.7 (in part of which the project lies). One of the designated  beneficial  uses is aquatic  life use: salmonid, spawning, rearing, and migration. Washington Department of Ecology's section 401 water quality  certification  required the licensee  attain the applicable temperature  standard for the Pend Oreille River for the protection of aquatic  life and states: "<emphasis typeStyle="it">Temperature  is not to exceed 1-DMax of 20.0 [degrees] C due to human activities. When natural conditions exceed 1-DMax of 20.0 [degrees] C, no temperature  increase will be allowed which raise the receiving water temperature  by greater than 0.3 [degrees] C; nor shall such temperature  increases, at any time, exceed t=34/(T+9)</emphasis>."</p></bodyText></footnote></p><p><page number="4" pageScheme="2013 FERC LEXIS 2031"/></p><p>5. The licensee  proposes to collect continuous water temperature  data from June to October, annually  at ten monitoring  locations in the Pend Oreille River, reservoir, and tributaries. The licensee  plans to monitor water temperature  using data loggers and manual field monitoring. </p><p>6. The licensee's  QAPP proposes quality control  protocols  for temperature  data and data management. Also, the licensee  proposes data verification and validation protocols  to ensure the quality and accuracy  of the water temperature  data are adequate.</p><p>7. The licensee  proposes to file an annual report  with Ecology detailing the implementation of riparian plantings, erosion stabilization, and other aquatic  habitat  enhancement  measures and results of monitoring  of these measures and temperature  data.</p><p>AGENCY CONSULTATION</p><p>8. On August 19, 2013, the licensee  submitted a draft of its QAPP for the Temperature  Attainment  Plan to the Washington Department of Ecology and the Boundary Water Quality  Work Group for their review and comment. The licensee  reports that no comments were received.</p><p>CONCLUSIONS</p><p>9. The licensee's  water temperature  monitoring,  data management and quality assurance <page number="5" pageScheme="2013 FERC LEXIS 2031"/> and control proposed in its QAPP should provide the necessary quantity and quality of data necessary to evaluate the effects of aquatic  habitat  improvements on water temperature  at the project. The plan should also provide adequate data to evaluate project water temperatures  in relation to temperature  attainment  goals defined in Ecology's WQC for the project.</p><p>10. The licensee  proposes to file an annual report  with Ecology summarizing the aquatic  habitat  improvements and temperature  data results, but does not propose a date or to file these annual reports with the Commission. Therefore, in order to keep the Commission informed of any project water temperature  improvements attained by the licensee's  habitat  improvement projects contained in the TAP, the licensee  should file a report annually  that includes licensee's  implementation of aquatic  habitat  enhancement  measures, data acquisition efforts, temperature  monitoring  results, and any other related information, with the Commission for review. Ecology and the WQWG should be allowed 30 days to review and comment on the report prior to the licensee  filing it with the Commission. The filing with the Commission should include any comments <page number="6" pageScheme="2013 FERC LEXIS 2031"/> received  from Ecology and the licensee's  responses to those comments and should be filed annually  by March 1. <footnote><anchor id="fnote4_25_584_15"/><label>4</label><bodyText><p> Since the licensee  proposes to conduct water temperature  sampling from June to October, the Commission's March 1 filing due date should allow the licensee  time to summarize  data, analyze any effects on aquatic  habitat  improvements and consult with the agencies regarding the monitoring  prior to filing the report with the Commission for review.</p></bodyText></footnote></p><p>11. The licensee's  filing satisfies the requirement of article 401(b) of the March 20, 2013, Order Issuing New License  for the Boundary Project, to develop a QAPP for its Temperature  Attainment  Plan and as modified,  should be approved.</p><p><emphasis typeStyle="un">The Director orders</emphasis>:</p><p>(A) The City of Seattle, Washington's (licensee)  Quality Assurance  Project Plan (QAPP) developed for its Temperature   Attainment  Plan, filed with the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission (Commission) on September 6, 2013, pursuant to license  Article 401(b) of the March 20, 2013, Order Issuing<page number="7" pageScheme="2013 FERC LEXIS 2031"/> New License  for the Boundary Project as modified  in paragraph (B) below, is approved.</p><p>(B) In order to keep the Commission informed of the results of the Temperature   Attainment  Plan and the water temperature   monitoring  effort, the licensee  shall file by March 1, an annual report  that summarizes  the implementation of the Temperature   Attainment  Plan and associated QAPP. The licensee  shall file this report after review by Washington Department of Ecology and the Water Quality  Work Group and shall also include any comments received  from both and the licensee's  responses to those comments.</p><p>(C) This order constitutes final agency action. Any party may file a request for rehearing of this order within 30 days from the date of its issuance,  as provided in <citation type="statute" id="I59XV8V92SF7MJ00C0000400"><content>section 313(a)</content></citation> of the Federal Power Act, <citation type="statute" id="I59XV8V92SF7MJ00D0000400"><content><span normalizedCite="16 U.S.C. ยง 825l">16 U.S.C. ยง 825<emphasis typeStyle="it">l</emphasis><locator><locatorKey><keyName name="citeReference"/><keyValue value="#1828#123#000016#       825l#"/></locatorKey></locator></span></content></citation> (2012), and the Commission's regulations at <citation type="regulation" id="I59XV8V92SF7MJ00F0000400"><content><span normalizedCite="18 C.F.R. ยง 385.713">18 C.F.R. ยง 385.713 (2013)<locator><locatorKey><keyName name="citeReference"/><keyValue value="#14829#123#000018#        385.713#"/></locatorKey></locator></span></content></citation>.
